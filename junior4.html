Hardware Repair

Junior Year Project #4

During junior year, I worked on multiple hardware repairs and teardown tasks to gain hands-on experience with disassembly, diagnostics, and component-level repair.

  • Disassembled a PS4 controller, removed the rumble motor, and soldered it back in place successfully.
  • Completely disassembled a Windows laptop to diagnose a DVD drive failure and Wi-Fi connectivity issue. I installed a new DVD drive and replaced the wireless card.
  • Opened a Chromebook to investigate and attempt bypassing the enterprise enrollment by disabling the write-protect screw (grounding screw).
  • Reassembled all devices, confirming functionality and learning safe teardown and reassembly practices.

These repairs helped me improve my confidence with small electronics, soldering, and internal diagnostics.
